Customer Segmentation Analysis

Project Overview

This project aimed to discover natural groupings in customer data to help a retail client develop targeted marketing strategies. By analyzing purchase history, demographics, and engagement metrics for over 50,000 customers, I identified distinct customer segments that could be approached with personalized marketing campaigns.

Using K-means clustering, the analysis revealed 5 distinct customer segments, each with unique characteristics and purchasing behaviors. The insights from this analysis helped the client develop targeted marketing strategies that resulted in a 24% increase in customer engagement and a 15% boost in sales for specific product categories.

The Challenge

The retail client faced several challenges that this project addressed:

  • Declining customer engagement despite increasing marketing spend
  • One-size-fits-all marketing approach not resonating with different customer types
  • Inability to identify high-value customers and their specific needs
  • No clear strategy for customer retention and loyalty programs

Approach & Methodology

Data Collection & Preparation

The dataset included transaction history, customer demographics, website interactions, and loyalty program data. Initial data preparation involved:

  • Cleaning missing values and outliers
  • Feature engineering to create metrics like purchase frequency, average order value, and recency
  • Normalizing variables to ensure equal weighting in the clustering algorithm

Exploratory Data Analysis

Before clustering, I conducted extensive exploratory analysis to understand patterns in the data:

  • Distribution analysis of key metrics (purchase frequency, spend, etc.)
  • Correlation analysis between variables
  • RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis

Clustering Approach

For the clustering, I implemented the following steps:

  1. Applied principal component analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality
  2. Used the elbow method to determine the optimal number of clusters
  3. Implemented K-means clustering with the optimal cluster count
  4. Validated clusters through silhouette analysis

Implementation Code

customer_segments = kmeans.fit_predict(X_scaled)

Results & Impact

The clustering analysis revealed 5 distinct customer segments:

Segment 1: High-Value Loyalists

12%

Frequent shoppers with high average order value and strong brand loyalty. Generate 35% of total revenue.

Segment 2: Potential Enthusiasts

23%

Medium purchase frequency with above-average spending. Good candidates for loyalty programs.

Segment 3: Occasional Buyers

31%

Infrequent shoppers with medium basket size. Respond well to seasonal promotions.

Segment 4: Bargain Hunters

24%

Price-sensitive customers who primarily shop during sales and with coupons.

Each segment required different marketing approaches:

  • High-Value Loyalists: VIP programs, early access to new products, personalized recommendations
  • Potential Enthusiasts: Loyalty rewards, cross-selling based on past purchases
  • Occasional Buyers: Seasonal campaigns, re-engagement emails
  • Bargain Hunters: Flash sales, discount-focused communication

Business Impact

Implementing targeted strategies based on these segments resulted in:

  • 24% increase in email engagement rates
  • 15% boost in sales for specific product categories
  • 31% improvement in loyalty program enrollment
  • 18% decrease in customer churn rate

Conclusion

This customer segmentation project demonstrates the power of data-driven marketing. By understanding distinct customer groups and their preferences, businesses can create more effective marketing strategies that resonate with their audience. The approach is scalable and can be applied to various industries beyond retail.

Future developments for this project could include:

  • Implementing real-time segmentation as customer behavior changes
  • Developing predictive models for each segment to anticipate future behavior
  • Creating automated recommendation systems tailored to each segment
