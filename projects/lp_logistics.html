Project Overview

As a Supply Chain Analytics Specialist at an Agricultural Export Company, I developed a multi-stage linear programming model to optimize our logistics network across Southeast Asia. The solution integrates production, domestic transportation, and international distribution into a unified decision framework, resulting in significant cost reductions and improved operational efficiency.

Business Context & Challenge

Our company operates four production facilities across Thailand and exports agricultural products to five key ASEAN markets. Previously, each supply chain stage was managed in isolation:

  • Production decisions were made without considering transportation constraints
  • Domestic transport routes were planned separately from international shipping
  • Intermodal hubs experienced either congestion or underutilization

This current approach resulted in:

  • Elevated transportation and warehousing costs
  • Frequent shipment delays affecting customer satisfaction
  • Inefficient use of logistics infrastructure
  • Difficulty meeting emissions reduction targets

Supply chain network visualization showing optimized routes between Thailand production facilities and ASEAN markets

Solution Approach

Although the ideal model would integrates all three stages into one cost-minimizing objective, practical implementation required decomposition into two sequential models for scalability and realistic decision making. Model 1 Production Optimization: Minimized manufacturing costs across four Thai production facilities while ensuring demand fulfillment and capacity constraints. Model 2 Routing Optimization: Using Model 1 output, this model minimized domestic and international transportation costs by determining optimal product flows through intermodal hubs to five ASEAN markets. It incorporated mode-specific constraints, hub capacities, and carbon emissions limits. This decomposed approach enabled adaptability to changing real-world parameters such as fuel prices and trade disruptions while significantly improving cost-efficiency and delivery reliability across the region.

Technical Implementation

1. Production Stage

  • Optimized production quantities across four facilities (Rayong, Ayutthaya, Chiang Mai, and Hat Yai)
  • Balanced manufacturing costs against transportation availability
  • Ensured production capacities were optimally utilized

2. Domestic Transportation Stage

  • Determined optimal product flow from production plants to intermodal hubs
  • Minimized transportation costs while considering hub capacity constraints
  • Reduced congestion and storage costs at intermodal facilities

Stage 1: Production Optimization

Decision Variables

  • xp,l = Quantity of product p produced at plant l

Objective Function

min ∑p,l (cp,lprod · xp,l)

Constraints

1. Factory Capacity

∑p xp,l ≤ Capl, ∀l

2. Meet Demand

∑l xp,l ≥ Dp, ∀p

Stage 2: Transportation Optimization

Decision Variables

  • yl,h = Amount transported from plant l to intermodal hub h
  • zh,c,m = Amount shipped from hub h to country c using transport mode m

Objective Function

min ∑l,h (cl,htrans · yl,h) + ∑h,c,m (ch,c,mship · zh,c,m)

Constraints

3. Flow Conservation at Hubs (Inbound = Outbound)

∑l yl,h = ∑c,m zh,c,m, ∀h

4. Hub Capacity Limits

∑p yl,h ≤ HCaph, ∀h

5. Mode-Specific Capacity Limits

zh,c,m ≤ MCaph,m, ∀h, c, m

6. Carbon Emissions Limit

∑h,c,m (em · zh,c,m) ≤ Emax

7. Link to Production Output (from Stage 1)

∑h yl,h = ∑p xp,l*, ∀l

Optimization Outcome

  • Stage 1 identifies cost-optimal production volumes per facility.
  • Stage 2 determines the most efficient logistics routes and modes under capacity and sustainability constraints.
  • This decomposition improves adaptability to dynamic conditions such as fuel prices or regulatory changes.

Results & Business Impact

The integrated supply chain optimization model generated significant insights across production allocation, transport mode selection, and cost efficiency. The visualization below shows how production is allocated across our four facilities:

Production allocation and capacity utilization across facilities

Production Allocation Insights

As shown in the chart, our production plan reveals:

  • Facility Specialization: Each facility has been assigned specific products to optimize production efficiency
  • Capacity Utilization: Rayong and Ayutthaya are operating at 100% capacity, while Chiang Mai (87.5%) and Hat Yai (45.8%) have some unused capacity
  • Product Distribution: Jasmine Rice is concentrated in Ayutthaya, Processed Seafood in Rayong, Palm Oil in Chiang Mai, and Rubber production is split between Rayong and Hat Yai

The optimization model also determined the most efficient transport modes for different destinations, balancing cost and emissions:

Transport mode selection and cost-emissions trade-off analysis

Transport Mode Selection Insights

Our transport analysis reveals several key patterns:

  • Mode Preferences: Ocean shipping dominates for most destinations due to its favorable cost-emissions profile
  • Air Transport: Manila receives almost all shipments via air transport, highlighting unique constraints or requirements for this market
  • Mixed Strategy: Jakarta uses a combination of ocean and air transport to balance time-sensitivity with cost efficiency
  • Emissions Consideration: As shown in the right chart, ocean transport provides the lowest emissions and costs per ton-km, while air transport has significantly higher values in both dimensions

Conclusion

This project demonstrates the power of data-driven optimization in managing complex supply chain networks. Key conclusions include:

  • Proven Value of Integrated Optimization: Without re-optimization, rising fuel costs would have increased overall expenses by 6.7%; with optimization, the increase was limited to just 0.5%
  • Measurable Cost Mitigation: The optimization model successfully minimized disruption impacts, delivering 5.8% cost savings compared to fixed logistics during a fuel surge
  • Strategic Mode Selection: The analysis revealed that ocean transport is the most efficient mode for most destinations, while air transport, despite higher costs, remains necessary for time-sensitive routes
  • Resilience Through Analytics: Dynamic optimization enables the supply chain to adapt to changing conditions, maintaining efficiency despite external disruptions

This approach ensures more cost-efficient, resilient, and sustainable logistics planning under dynamic market conditions.
