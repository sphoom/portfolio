Supply Chain Network Optimization
Integrating Production, Transportation & Distribution

Project Overview

As a Supply Chain Analytics Specialist at an Agricultural Export Company, I developed a multi-stage linear programming model to optimize our logistics network across Southeast Asia. The solution integrates production, domestic transportation, and international distribution into a unified decision framework, resulting in significant cost reductions and improved operational efficiency.

Business Context & Challenge

Our company operates four production facilities across Thailand and exports agricultural products to five key ASEAN markets. Previously, each supply chain stage was managed in isolation:

  • Production decisions were made without considering transportation constraints
  • Domestic transport routes were planned separately from international shipping
  • Intermodal hubs experienced either congestion or underutilization

This siloed approach resulted in:

  • Elevated transportation and warehousing costs
  • Frequent shipment delays affecting customer satisfaction
  • Inefficient use of logistics infrastructure
  • Difficulty meeting emissions reduction targets

Solution Approach

I developed an integrated optimization model that simultaneously considers all three interdependent stages of our supply chain:

1. Production Stage

  • Optimized production quantities across four facilities (Rayong, Ayutthaya, Chiang Mai, and Hat Yai)
  • Balanced manufacturing costs against transportation availability
  • Ensured production capacities were optimally utilized

2. Domestic Transportation Stage

  • Determined optimal product flow from production plants to intermodal hubs
  • Minimized transportation costs while considering hub capacity constraints
  • Reduced congestion and storage costs at intermodal facilities

3. International Distribution Stage

  • Selected optimal shipping modes (ocean, road, air) for each destination market
  • Accounted for tariffs, transit times, and carbon footprints
  • Ensured compliance with emissions policies while minimizing costs

Technical Implementation

I formulated this complex challenge as a linear programming model that:

  • Objective: Minimizes total costs across all three supply chain stages
  • Decision Variables: Determines production quantities, domestic transport volumes, and international shipping allocations
  • Key Constraints: Respects factory capacities, ensures demand fulfillment, maintains flow conservation, and adheres to emissions limits

Decision Variables

Let:

  • Productionp,l = Quantity produced at each plant l for each product p
  • Transportl,h = Amount transported from each plant l to an intermodal hub h
  • Shiph,c,m = Amount shipped from hub h to country c using transport mode m

Objective Function: Minimize Total Cost

min ∑(p,l) (ProductionCost_{p,l} · Production_{p,l}) 
            + ∑(l,h) (TransportCost_{l,h} · Transport_{l,h}) 
            + ∑(h,c,m) (ShippingCost_{h,c,m} · Ship_{h,c,m})

where:

  • ProductionCost includes manufacturing expenses
  • TransportCost includes domestic trucking costs
  • ShippingCost includes ocean freight, air transport, and tariffs

Constraints

(A) Production Constraints

1. Factory Capacity Limits

∑p Productionp,l ≤ FactoryCapacityl, ∀l

2. Meet Demand

∑l Productionp,l ≥ Demandp, ∀p

(B) Domestic Transportation Constraints

3. Flow Conservation at Hubs (Inbound = Outbound)

∑l Transportl,h = ∑c,m Shiph,c,m, ∀h

4. Hub Capacity Limits

∑p Transportl,h ≤ HubCapacityh, ∀h

(C) International Shipping Constraints

5. Mode-Specific Capacity Limits

Shiph,c,m ≤ ModeCapacityh,m, ∀h, c, m

6. Carbon Emissions Limit

∑h,c,m (CO2Emissionm · Shiph,c,m) ≤ EmissionLimit

Optimization Outcome

Solving this multi-stage LP model provides:

  • Cost-optimal production and transportation decisions
  • The best transport mode mix to minimize fuel costs and emissions
  • Adaptability to real-world constraints (e.g., fuel price surge, trade policies)

Strategic Decision Support Through Scenario Analysis

To address key strategic questions from management, I leveraged the optimization model to provide actionable insights:

1. Optimized Standard Scenario

"What is our optimal network configuration under current conditions?"

  • Established the cost-optimized baseline for production allocation and transport routing
  • Identified existing bottlenecks and opportunities for immediate improvement

2. Risk Mitigation Planning

"How would we adapt if fuel prices surge by 30%?"

  • Simulated optimal network reconfiguration in response to significant fuel cost increases
  • Identified alternate transport modes and routes to minimize cost impact
  • Developed contingency plans for operations teams

3. Cost-Benefit Analysis

"What cost savings can we achieve through dynamic optimization versus fixed logistics plans?"

  • Quantified the financial benefit of adaptive optimization compared to maintaining fixed logistics decisions
  • Demonstrated the value of integrated planning across all supply chain stages

Key Insights

  • Transport Cost Analysis: The 30% fuel surge directly increased transport costs, which remained unchanged even after re-optimization due to operational constraints
  • Storage Optimization Success: Strategic redistribution of shipments and inventory allocation delivered 15.31% savings in storage costs, demonstrating significant value
  • Optimization Under Constraints: The model revealed that while some cost elements (transport) had limited flexibility, others (storage) provided substantial optimization opportunities

Conclusion

This project demonstrates the power of data-driven optimization in managing complex supply chain networks. Key conclusions include:

  • Proven Value of Integrated Optimization: Without re-optimization, rising fuel costs would have directly increased overall expenses across the network
  • Measurable Cost Mitigation: The optimization model successfully minimized disruption impacts, particularly by reducing storage costs by 15.31%
  • Resilience Through Analytics: Strategic transshipment planning enabled by this model provides a systematic approach to mitigating external disruptions

This approach ensures more cost-efficient, resilient, and sustainable logistics planning under dynamic market conditions.
